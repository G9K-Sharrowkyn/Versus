## 0. Hook

This is a tempo fight, not a simple brick mirror. New 52 Superman owns the cleaner speed, cleaner decisions, and cleaner first minute. King Hyperion is the stickier survival profile: if Clark does not turn early control into a real finish, regeneration and brutality start erasing the value of every good sequence.

## 2. Character introduction

### 2.1 Superman

Superman is the higher-tempo control fighter here. He has better spacing, faster tactical adaptation, and more ways to create a non-lethal or decisive finish before attrition becomes the real game. His best route is to close the fight before Hyperion's regeneration economics matter.

### 2.2 King Hyperion

King Hyperion is the uglier collision fighter. He wants direct contact, repeated trades, and a long enough exchange that raw survival and regeneration start flattening Superman's cleaner advantages. His best route is to make every sequence feel unfinished.

## 4. Origin and profile

Superman is the better problem-solver and the faster starter. Hyperion is the dirtier attrition threat, more willing to eat damage to force a lethal exchange. That contrast is the whole fight: one side wins by solving the problem early, the other wins by making the problem stay unsolved.

## 5. Powers and weaknesses

### Superman

Superman brings speed, flight, heat vision, freeze breath, and high-end sensory processing. His toolkit lets him stack offense, reposition instantly, and create cleaner incap or BFR routes than Hyperion can. `Solar Flare` is the important escalation point: it is a real native finisher path for this version, but it is costly enough that Clark cannot treat it like a free opener.

### King Hyperion

Hyperion brings atomic vision, brute-force contact pressure, and extreme regeneration. The molecular-vibration utility matters only as a secondary survival or escape wrinkle, not as the main win condition. Argonite and mental instability exist in continuity, but they are not central unless the fight drags into exactly the wrong kind of exchange for him.
Supporting ceiling note: the local `Exiles` evidence also shows Hyperion overwhelming two alternate Hyperions and explicitly claiming he killed Thor, Hercules, and Galactus in his ruined home reality. That Galactus line is statement-backed rather than an on-panel direct win, so it raises his ceiling without replacing the cleaner showcased feats and victories below.

### Swing factor

The real swing factor is whether Superman can convert early momentum into a real finish before Hyperion's regeneration economics and attrition pressure erase the tempo edge.

## 8. Hypothetical fight - step by step

### Opening

Superman should own the first read. He is faster, he spaces better, and he has the cleaner options for turning movement into immediate positional advantage. Hyperion's correct answer is the same answer he always wants: eat the first sequence if he has to, close distance, and make the fight violent enough that pure tempo stops mattering.

### Mid-fight

The key question is not whether Superman can hurt Hyperion. He clearly can. The real question is whether he can close the fight before regeneration starts resetting the value of every good sequence. If Clark keeps the exchange technical, layered, and mobile, the fight trends toward him. If Hyperion keeps surviving into the next contact, the economics change.

### Turning points

- The first real Superman incap attempt decides whether Hyperion is just pressured or actually endangered.
- The first Hyperion survival through a sequence that should have ended most peers changes the fight from tempo control to closure stress.
- Mismanaged beam exchanges can punish Hyperion, especially if Superman changes angle and forces bad atomic-vision alignment.
- The biggest escalation point is whether Superman commits to `Solar Flare` or keeps trying to solve the fight with cleaner, lower-cost control.

- **Explicit:** ComicVine's direct matchup thread, `CaV Sover-New 52 Superman VS TGW-King Hyperion`, leans toward the Superman side because speed application and cleaner proof quality are easier to defend than Hyperion's regen-heavy but patchier top-end record. - **Derived:** The feat synthesis still points the same way. Superman owns the better start, the better control game, and the better non-lethal finish paths; Hyperion's real route is to make the fight last long enough that the first three advantages stop converting into closure.

## 9. Minority perspective

The strongest pro-Hyperion counter is simple: regeneration plus ruthlessness can punish any Superman who does not commit fast enough to a real finishing route. If Clark keeps winning exchanges without ending the fight, Hyperion is exactly the kind of opponent who can make that look like a mistake.

## 10. Base verdict

**Winner: Superman (New 52) - 7/10**

Superman takes the base matchup because he has the cleaner speed edge, the cleaner tactical layer, and the better chance to convert early control into KO, BFR, or an escalation finish. Hyperion is dangerous because he survives well enough to make the fight uglier than the averages suggest, but the default shape still favors Clark.

## 11. Conditional verdict

1. **Solar Flare + Standard KO/BFR/Incap**  
   **Superman 7/10.** This is his best branch. He already owns tempo, and the legal finisher route gives him the cleanest way to stop Hyperion before regeneration fully compounds.

2. **No Solar Flare + Standard KO/BFR/Incap**  
   **Superman 6/10.** Clark still has the better control game, but he now has to win through cleaner incap or removal rather than a harder endbeat, which gives Hyperion more room to stay live.

3. **Solar Flare + Kill-only Deathmatch**  
   **Superman 5.5/10.** Solar Flare keeps a real kill path on the table, but the cost of using it and the certainty required in a kill-only structure keep this from becoming comfortable.

4. **No Solar Flare + Kill-only Deathmatch**  
   **King Hyperion 6/10.** This is Hyperion's best branch. If Superman has to solve regeneration permanently without the nastiest built-in finisher, the fight starts drifting toward the attrition specialist.

## 12. Why stats and verdict can diverge

Superman's better profile does not erase regeneration economics. Hyperion's better attrition branch does not erase the speed gap. The reason the paper still leans Superman is that this matchup is decided by closure quality: who can turn their advantage into an actual ending before the other side's preferred fight shape becomes permanent.

## 13. Closing

This is not a stomp. It is a control-versus-attrition fight where the cleaner toolkit belongs to Superman and the stickier survival branch belongs to Hyperion. The biggest uncertainty is not whether Hyperion can take damage, but how often Superman chooses or needs the truly decisive finish path before repeated exchanges make the fight worse for him.
