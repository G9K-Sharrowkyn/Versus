Thanos was born on Saturn's moon Titan as the son of Eternals A'lars and Sui-San; his brother is Eros of Titan. Thanos carries the Deviants gene, and as such, shares the physical appearance of the Eternals' cousin race. Shocked by his appearance and the belief that he would destroy all life in the universe, Sui-San attempted to kill him, but she was stopped by A'lars. During his school years, Thanos was a pacifist and would only play with his brother Eros and pets. By adolescence, Thanos had become fascinated with nihilism and death, worshiping and eventually falling in love with the physical embodiment of death, Mistress Death. As an adult, Thanos augmented his physical strength and powers through his superior scientific knowledge. He also attempted to create a new life for himself by siring many children as well as becoming a pirate. He finds no fulfillment in either until he is visited again by Mistress Death, for whom he murders his offspring and his pirate captain. Wishing to impress Mistress Death, Thanos gathers an army of villainous aliens and begins a nuclear bombardment of Titan that kills millions of his race, and then begins search of more power in order to fulfill his goal of spreading death and destruction through the universe for his love.

Quantum Uncertainty: Due to the precarious nature of his state of being. The Mad Titan possesses a rather obtuse constitution that posits him square outside the astrological norm, even by Titan standards.[41] This means he is not limited to the constraining physical and supertropical confines of the cosmos nor by its voluminous bylaws that keep reality percolating.[190] It is through this cataphysical oddity of his existential being that not only is Thanos incapable of ever truly dying, but is also capable of attesting towards anything he pushes his will above and beyond the limit of testament.[33]
Freedom: Due in part to being an anomaly of the Eternals, having been conceived outside of conventional fabrication.[31] Thanos's oddity of being means he is unmoored from the natural perpetuance of causality, being immune to the constants of life, death and fate entails the Mad Titan true autonomy from everything that is.[22] A being living "Beyond the Norm" as Thanos describes himself, with his power and will going above and outside the pan-reality firmament comprising the higher-cosmic uniformity, means he is free of the constraints of destiny, continuity, reality and even the most absurd of impossibilities, in all their perspective's or uncertainties.[191] No matter what in whichever form of multiversal to quisiversal existentiality, Thanos cannot be unmade or remade into anything other than who and/or what he really is. Should he physically be extinguished, he will always return,[15][160] having been disintegrated and cast into outer oblivion, he compelled himself back into existence from the yawning void.[6] With or without outside help, the Titanian will always and forever be as he is, for only Thanos is indefinite.[41]

Titanian Eternal Physiology: By far the strongest and most powerful Eternal from Titan, if not in general, Thanos is a superpowered mutant whose massive, heavy-bodied form was born with the capacity to synthesize cosmic energy for certain personal uses. He was deemed a Category 1 Life Ender by the Nova Corps, to be designated a "universal" threat level and to have been a one-time reality ender.[192] Being seemingly categorized as a Cosmic-level threat, he was sent by the Shi'ar Imperial Guard to the core cell of a galactic gulag.[141]

Thanos punched Thane into the planet they were fighting above and destroyed it by charging through him.[146]

His agility is roughly equal to that of the average Eternal.[citation needed] He was able to dodge a Power Gem enhanced Champion of the Universe.[33]
Superhuman Reflexes: Thanos' reaction time is enhanced to a level that is beyond the natural physical limits of the finest human athlete. Like his agility, his reflexes are about average for an Eternal.

He possesses such an incredible resistance to injury that he has been shown to survive point-blank blasts from Odin (including blasts that staggered but didn't cause damage nor knocked him down),[81] and Galactus.[198] Thanos has withstood three full screams from Black Bolt, one at point-blank range, without any significant damage aside from damaging his suit,[111] survived being subjected to an artificial 2-light year wide black hole,[199] and withstood being stabbed in the chest by Wolverine's Adamantium claws without exhibiting any discomfort.[200] Not even the specially crafted Godslayer blade could penetrate the mad titan's toughened skin and fibers when applying it to stop his rampage. Gamora herself stating it was made to kill the divine, yet the blade shattered when she used it on her adoptive father.[97]
