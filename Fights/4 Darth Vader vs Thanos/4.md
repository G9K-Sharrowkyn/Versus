Darth Vader (Star Wars Legends Prime) vs. Thanos (Marvel Comics base, no Infinity Stones/Gauntlet)

Thanos holds a clear but not overwhelming edge in raw physical power, durability, and regenerative capacity, making him the likely winner in most neutral engagements (research suggests ~7/10 odds).
Vader’s advantages in reaction speed, lightsaber mastery, and versatile Force hax (choke, precognition, telekinesis) create credible upset paths, especially if he exploits early distance or mental attacks.
On-paper stats favor Thanos slightly (overall ~90 vs. Vader’s ~87), but the matchup hinges on whether Vader’s internal Force effects bypass Thanos’s cosmic physiology and regen.
Outcome appears stable across standard deathmatch assumptions on a neutral battlefield; no major rule changes flip the winner decisively.
Key swing factor: Thanos’s matter manipulation and energy projection can end the fight quickly if Vader cannot maintain distance or land a decisive hax early.

Character Profiles
Darth Vader (Legends Prime) is the armored Dark Lord at his ROTJ-era peak: a relentless Djem So duelist who augments physical limits with the Force, relying on precognition, crushing telekinesis, and mind-affecting powers while sustaining catastrophic injury through sheer will.
Thanos (base 616, no stones) is the Mad Titan: an Eternal-enhanced brawler with planet-shaking strength, near-indestructible durability, energy blasts, matter transmutation, telepathy, and rapid regeneration, backed by millennia of conquest and cosmic warfare experience.

0. Hook
In one corner stands the galaxy’s most feared enforcer, a cybernetic Sith whose every breath is fueled by rage and whose blade has felled legions of Jedi. In the other, the Mad Titan—conqueror of worlds, survivor of black holes, and physical equal to heralds of the cosmos. Raw power versus surgical precision; immortal resilience versus dark-side sorcery. This is not a clash of equals on paper, yet the Force has toppled greater odds before.

2. Character introduction
2.1 Darth Vader (Legends Prime)
Archetype: armored Sith juggernaut. Style: aggressive Djem So with seamless Force integration; mentality: cold tactical predator who anticipates strikes via precognition and never yields. Strongest edges: relativistic reactions, versatile telekinesis/mind hax, lightsaber that slices nearly any matter, and dark-side stamina that lets him fight for hours while mortally wounded.
2.2 Thanos (base Marvel Comics)
Archetype: cosmic warlord/brawler. Style: overwhelming physical power blended with energy projection and matter manipulation; mentality: calculating genius who out-thinks abstracts and refuses to stay down. Strongest edges: planet-crushing strength, near-indestructible durability with high-end regeneration, telepathy/resistance, and energy blasts capable of one-shotting herald-level foes.

4. Origin and profile
Vader’s philosophy is domination through fear and precision; he studies opponents, exploits weaknesses, and turns pain into power. Thanos embodies ruthless efficiency and long-term calculation—he has humbled cosmic entities through strategy as often as brute force. Both are patient hunters, but Thanos’s millennia of galactic conquest give him broader tactical depth against unknown physiologies, while Vader’s intimate knowledge of Force-weaknesses offers surgical counters.
5. Powers and weaknesses
Vader: Lightsaber (plasma edge cuts most materials), Force choke/push/wave, precognition, telepathy/mind tricks, illusions, Sith sorcery, dark-side healing/stamina. Weaknesses: suit can be overloaded or deactivated; physical body is crippled without Force support.
Thanos: Superhuman strength/durability, energy projection (eye beams, omni-blasts), matter transmutation, telepathy, force fields, teleportation (self/others), regeneration from near-skeletal state. Weaknesses: not a dedicated speedster; can be briefly stunned by overwhelming internal attacks.
Highest-impact swing factor: whether Vader’s Force choke or mental assault can bypass Thanos’s cosmic physiology and TP resistance before Thanos closes distance and lands a single decisive strike.

8. Hypothetical fight – step by step
8.1 Opening
Vader seizes initiative with precognitive burst, closing or maintaining distance while launching Force chokes and telekinetic waves. Thanos tanks the initial assault with casual durability, immediately countering with energy blasts or closing the gap via raw speed and teleportation feints. Vader controls early tempo via superior reactions and lightsaber defense.
8.2 Mid-fight
As distance closes, Thanos’s physicality grows dominant; Vader’s lightsaber glances off Thanos’s skin or is blocked by force fields while Vader dodges planet-crushing punches. Stamina favors Thanos in prolonged brawling; Vader’s dark-side regen keeps him in the fight longer than expected, but Thanos adapts by using matter manipulation to disrupt Vader’s suit or telepathy to counter mental probes.
8.3 Turning points

Vader lands full-power Force choke—trigger: early distance; beneficiary: Vader if it bypasses physiology (impact: stuns or internals damage).
Thanos lands clean energy blast or punch—trigger: Vader overextends; beneficiary: Thanos (impact: suit overload or instant KO).
Thanos teleports behind for ambush—beneficiary: Thanos (impact: ends fight before Vader’s speed matters).
Vader’s illusion/mental assault confuses Thanos momentarily—beneficiary: Vader (impact: opens window for lightsaber decapitation attempt).
