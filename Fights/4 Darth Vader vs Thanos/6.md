## 0. Hook

Darth Vader at his Legends peak is a nightmare for most sci-fi fighters: precise telekinesis, elite dueling skill, strong battlefield control, and a real internal-attack threat. Thanos without the Infinity Stones is not "most fighters." This matchup is defined by one ugly question: can Vader create a lethal opening before he learns that Thanos lives on a durability tier his normal kill routes do not naturally solve?

## 2. Character introduction

### 2.1 Darth Vader (Legends Prime)
Vader fights like a pressure technician trapped inside a siege engine. His best practical tools are telekinesis, choke/crush routes, precognition, and a lightsaber game that punishes mistakes instantly. Against most opponents that creates a terrifying first-exchange advantage. His problem here is scale: he is built to break elite Jedi, assassins, monsters, and military hardware, not to erase a cosmic tank that regularly eats attacks from Marvel heavyweights.

### 2.2 Thanos (Earth-616, no Infinity Stones)
Base Thanos is still a cosmic warlord who mixes absurd physical power with energy projection, shielding, matter manipulation, and elite mental resistance. He does not need the Gauntlet to be a matchup problem. The practical issue for Vader is that Thanos has too many ways to survive the opening, too much physical mass behind every answer, and too much experience fighting people whose output already sits far above what Vader usually solves.

## 4. Origin and profile

Vader is a fallen Jedi built around domination through pressure, fear, and perfect punishment. He is disciplined, brutal, and much more tactically flexible than his image suggests. Thanos is colder. He is not just strong; he is comfortable fighting people with god-tier output, adjusting mid-exchange, and winning ugly if the clean route is not there. Vader enters this fight needing a surgical answer. Thanos enters it needing to survive that first answer and then turn the map against a smaller, more fragile opponent.

## 5. Powers and weaknesses

### Darth Vader
- Tools: lightsaber lethality, telekinesis, Force choke/crush, precognition, energy defense, and multi-vector combat control.
- Best swing factor: internal-force offense that can bypass ordinary external durability and create Vader's only credible upset path.
- Main weakness: life-support dependence and a far lower durability scale than Thanos.

### Thanos
- Tools: cosmic-tier strength, absurd durability, energy projection, force fields, telepathy resistance, matter manipulation, and battlefield control.
- Best swing factor: layered durability plus more than one answer once Vader's first bypass attempt fails.
- Main weakness: psychological arrogance more than a real physical gap.

## 8. Hypothetical fight - step by step

### 8.1 Opening
Vader has the cleaner first read. He enters faster, angles better, and brings immediate force-based threat instead of waiting for a long setup. The opening is therefore about conversion: can that precision create real damage before Thanos realizes he does not have to respect Vader like a normal duel opponent? Thanos does not need a perfect start. He only needs to survive the first serious test.

### 8.2 Mid-fight
This is where the matchup usually breaks. Vader layers telekinesis, choke pressure, footwork, and saber threat, looking for a bypass route before the gap becomes physical. Thanos survives the first round of ideas, understands that the force layer is not instantly decisive, and stops treating the exchange like a technical duel. From there the fight turns into shields, beams, brute-force counters, and direct body punishment against a much smaller combatant whose suit is not built for repeated cosmic-level answers.

### 8.3 Turning points
1. **Internal-force test:** If Vader's choke/crush route fails to create immediate meaningful damage, his best shortcut disappears.
2. **First clean Thanos answer:** Once Thanos lands a real physical or energy-based counter, Vader's margin for error drops from small to almost zero.
3. **Control-layer collapse:** Vader can shape space, but he cannot hold Thanos at a safe range forever if Thanos simply powers through the control.
4. **Resistance check:** If Thanos resists mental disruption and internal pressure well enough to keep functioning, Vader is forced back into a losing stat war.

### 8.4 Source verdict distribution
- **ComicVine battle thread - explicit:** Thanos favored heavily. Link: https://comicvine.gamespot.com/forums/battles-7/darth-vader-vs-thanos-602364/ Short note: even direct battleboard discussion treats the fight as one-sided because Vader's technical edge does not solve the scale gap.
- **Feat-source synthesis - derived:** Respect-thread evidence favors Thanos clearly. Vader's best proof supports elite control, timing, and bypass attempts; Thanos's best proof supports surviving those attempts and overpowering a much higher class of opponent than Vader usually faces.

## 9. Minority perspective
The strongest pro-Vader argument is simple: he does not need to outmuscle Thanos if he can bypass him. A perfect internal-force attack chained into immediate saber damage is the one real upset theory worth taking seriously. The problem is concrete, not abstract: Thanos has too much evidence of surviving internal stress, overwhelming energy, and hax-heavy cosmic opposition, while Vader has too little evidence of actually finishing a target from this tier before the counterattack arrives. The route exists in theory. It is just too thin in practice.

## 10. Base verdict
**Winner:** Thanos

**Score:** Thanos 9/10

Vader is faster to the first meaningful decision and more technical in a pure fighting sense. Thanos is still too strong, too durable, too layered, and too proven against opponents from a much higher bracket. The cleanest summary is simple: Vader has a narrow opening-based win condition. Thanos owns everything that happens if that opening fails.

## 12. Why stats and verdict can diverge
Vader's technical edge is real. It is the reason the opening does not look completely hopeless. But technical superiority only matters if it creates a finish route, and against Thanos it mostly changes the texture of the first exchange rather than the outcome of the whole fight. That is why Vader can look better in some categories and still lose the matchup badly.

## 13. Closing
Darth Vader is the better pure fighter. Thanos is the wrong kind of opponent for that to matter enough. The scan-backed picture is consistent from multiple angles: Vader can control moments, threaten with hax, and punish mistakes, but Thanos operates on a durability-and-power tier that turns those advantages into short-term problems rather than fight-ending solutions. This remains a stable Thanos matchup unless you inflate Vader's bypass tools beyond what the best proof actually supports.
