10. Character A Powers / Tools / Weaknesses
- Powers: Explosive starting speed, reflex ahead of sight, total body coordination.
- Tools: Jeet Kune Do (Interception concept), low kicks, finger strikes, breaking the opponent's leg structure.
- Weaknesses: Massive mass deficit (approx. 60kg vs Ali's 100kg) and reach.
- Weaknesses: Lack of experience in multi-round wars of attrition with elite heavyweights.

11. Character A Raw Feats
- "One-Inch Punch" demonstration at Long Beach (generating massive force from minimal distance).
- Two-finger push-ups and superhuman body control.
- 1958 inter-school boxing title (confirmed technical base).
- Creation of Jeet Kune Do as a system based on practical effectiveness over tradition.

12. Character B Powers / Tools / Weaknesses
- Powers: Lightweight speed in a giant's body, incredible punch resistance (granite chin).
- Tools: Range-controlling jab, "Ali Shuffle," Rope-a-dope technique, elite clinch.
- Weaknesses: Toolkit limited to boxing—Ali never had to defend against knee kicks or takedowns.
- Weaknesses: Confidence bordering on bravado, which Bruce could exploit for a counter.

13. Character B Raw Feats
- "Rope-a-dope" against Foreman (absorbing lethal punches and surviving the onslaught).
- Peak speed and precision against Williams (the fastest hands in heavyweight history).
- Endurance in the Frazier wars (fighting to the limit in extreme conditions).
- Footwork allowing him to dodge dozens of punches without a guard.
