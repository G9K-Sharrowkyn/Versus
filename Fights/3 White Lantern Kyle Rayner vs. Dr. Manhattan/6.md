## 0. Hook
White Lantern Kyle Rayner is the cleaner profile on paper: faster active pressure, broader combat tools, and a rewrite path that can reach outside normal construct logic. Dr. Manhattan is the opposite kind of threat. He does not need to win the stat race if he can attack the setup before Kyle's best option fully exists. That tension defines the whole matchup.

## 1. Introduction and methodology
This paper uses a scan-first source ladder: Reddit respect threads first, ComicVine feat/respect threads second, and DC Fandom only for issue lookup and continuity checks. Locked versions are White Lantern Kyle Rayner (New 52 / Prime Earth, Green Lantern: New Guardians era, with Life Equation access) and Dr. Manhattan (Watchmen / Rebirth / Doomsday Clock continuity role). Stats use the project's nine matchup-relative categories on a 1-100 scale. Sections 6 and 7 are curated showcase lists, not exhaustive dumps.

## 2. Character introduction
### 2.1 White Lantern Kyle Rayner
Kyle fights like a pressure-builder. He layers constructs, movement, and metaphysical versatility until the board starts obeying his side of the matchup. His best route is not a clean brawl. It is forcing a real action window and then escalating into White Light or Life Equation rewrite before the opponent collapses the setup.

### 2.2 Dr. Manhattan
Manhattan does not need normal combat rhythm. He reads the encounter from outside linear exchange, attacks the mechanism of a move rather than the finished attack, and turns fights into questions of preemption and deconstruction. Against setup-dependent opponents, that first-move asymmetry is often more important than their broader toolkit.

## 4. Origin and profile
Kyle is a flexible combat creator who gets stronger as the exchange becomes more layered. Manhattan is a detached preemptor who is strongest before normal symmetry exists at all. Kyle wants the fight to become a contest of options. Manhattan wants to stop it from ever becoming that kind of fight.

## 5. Powers and weaknesses
Kyle brings White Lantern spectrum access, construct versatility, healing, teleportation, life-force manipulation, and a real Life Equation rewrite route. The weakness is not raw lack of power. It is dependence on a ring-mediated setup and on getting enough room to manifest the best version of his win condition.

Manhattan brings atomic control, direct disintegration, self-reassembly, non-linear perception, and timeline-level interference. His practical edge is that he can attack the source or timing of a move instead of just tanking it. The one real swing factor is whether Kyle can force a metaphysical rewrite window before Manhattan targets the ring, the timeline of the action, or the entire setup state itself.

## 8. Hypothetical fight - step by step
### 8.1 Opening
Kyle tries to take initiative with speed, layered constructs, and immediate White Light pressure. Manhattan does not need to trade into that rhythm. He targets the setup itself: the ring, the timing of the move, or the state of the field before Kyle's best option matures.

### 8.2 Mid-fight
If Kyle survives that first asymmetry, the fight becomes dangerous for Manhattan fast. White Light versatility plus Life Equation access means Kyle does not need to win through raw damage. He only needs one real rewrite window. Manhattan, in turn, tries to stop the fight from ever reaching that symmetrical phase.

### 8.3 Turning points
- The first construct layer that Manhattan fails to preempt is the first moment Kyle can turn the fight into a real exchange.
- The first direct attack on the ring source or the move-timeline decides whether Kyle is fighting with his full toolkit or just fragments of it.
- If Kyle reaches full Life Equation expression before Manhattan collapses the setup, the whole matchup flips from deletion pressure to rewrite pressure.
- If Manhattan keeps the fight in the preemptive phase, Kyle's superior averages never cash out into a stable finishing sequence.

### 8.4 Source verdict distribution
- **explicit:** The current folder baseline already frames the matchup as "Kyle wins the stats, Manhattan leads the fight," which is the clearest direct read of the version-locked setup used here.
- **derived:** The scan-backed feat synthesis points the same way: Kyle has the broader active toolkit, but Manhattan has the better first move and the cleaner route to stopping the setup before it becomes a rewrite war.

## 9. Minority perspective
The anti-consensus view is real. Kyle is not a fake underdog here. He is the side with the better paper profile, better combat rhythm, and a genuine metaphysical overwrite path. If Manhattan hesitates, observes too long, or fails to hit the setup before full Life Equation access manifests, Kyle has the tools to flip the whole state of the fight at once.

## 10. Base verdict
**Dr. Manhattan 6.5/10.** Kyle is better on paper and broader in live combat, but Manhattan has the better default starting position. In a matchup built around setup versus preemption, the side that can erase the setup first has the more reliable baseline.

## 11. Conditional verdict
1. **Life Equation: Full | Manhattan: Proactive** ? **Dr. Manhattan 6.5/10.** Full rewrite access keeps Kyle live, but proactive preemption still steals enough openings to keep Manhattan narrowly ahead.
2. **Life Equation: Limited | Manhattan: Proactive** ? **Dr. Manhattan 9/10.** If the rewrite route is throttled and Manhattan acts first, Kyle loses the only branch that really changes the fight.
3. **Life Equation: Full | Manhattan: Passive** ? **Kyle 7/10.** This is the branch where the stats finally matter. If Manhattan allows a real action window and Kyle reaches full rewrite expression, Kyle can seize the fight outright.
4. **Life Equation: Limited | Manhattan: Passive** ? **Dr. Manhattan 6.5/10.** Passivity narrows the gap, but limited Life Equation access still leaves Kyle too dependent on a setup Manhattan can eventually unravel.

## 12. Why stats and verdict can diverge
Kyle wins more categories because he is the more complete combat profile. Manhattan wins the default fight more often because he attacks the timing and infrastructure of actions, not just the visible attack. Better stats help only after the fight becomes symmetrical, and Manhattan's whole game is preventing that state.

## 13. Closing
This matchup is not about who has the prettier average. It is about whether Kyle gets to become the version of himself that the stat table is measuring before Manhattan edits the board out from under him. Kyle has the better profile. Manhattan has the better default reality of the fight. The largest uncertainty is simple: how often full Life Equation access actually arrives before preemption lands.
