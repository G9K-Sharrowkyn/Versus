## 0. Hook
This fight is not about who has the bigger raw aura. It is about whether Trigon can turn the battlefield into a demonic rule set before Galactus turns that same hostility into usable fuel. Galactus is the steadier default engine. Trigon is the more dangerous branch character whenever hunger and home-realm logic line up.

## 2. Character introduction
### 2.1 Trigon
Trigon fights by making the arena itself hostile. His best routes are corruption, possession, and domain pressure that break normal combat symmetry before the opponent can stabilize the exchange. He is most dangerous when the battlefield already behaves like an extension of his will.

### 2.2 Galactus
Galactus fights like a cosmic engine, not a brawler. His pressure comes from scale, barriers, transmutation, and the ability to turn hostile environments into solvable energy problems. He is strongest when the field can be metabolized rather than respected.

## 4. Origin and profile
Trigon is an escalating corruption engine. He wants the fight to stop behaving like a normal exchange and start behaving like a demonic occupation. Galactus is the opposite: a larger, steadier cosmic system that prefers to normalize chaos into energy, pressure, and attrition. The matchup is not "bigger stats versus smaller stats." It is "can the realm stay poisonous long enough to matter?"

## 5. Powers and weaknesses
### Trigon
Trigon brings Heart of Darkness empowerment, corruption, possession, dimensional travel, realm control, and fast regeneration. His best control is strongest when the battlefield already favors him. On neutral ground he is still dangerous, but his hax has less environmental support and more pressure to land early.

### Galactus
Galactus brings Power Cosmic, transmutation, barriers, energy projection, energy absorption, and conversion at scale. His safest routes are better when he is well-fed and can treat the whole field as part of his economy. Lower-fed states do not erase his threat, but they make his cleanest answers less reliable and give Trigon more room to force a hostile exchange.

### Swing factor
The whole fight turns on whether Galactus can convert Trigon's hostile field into usable energy before Trigon locks the exchange into demonic control logic.

## 8. Hypothetical fight - step by step
### 8.1 Opening
Trigon tries to turn the first minute into corruption space: portals, soul pressure, hostile atmosphere, and early anti-symmetry. Galactus answers by stabilizing scale and energy economy instead of sprinting into a direct exchange. The opening question is not "who hits harder first?" It is "does the field become demonic before it becomes metabolizable?"

### 8.2 Mid-fight
If Trigon's first corruption windows fail to stick, Galactus starts reframing the battlefield as an energy problem rather than an occult one. That is the phase where paper parity stops mattering and field logic takes over. If the realm stays poisonous, Trigon's hax remains live. If Galactus starts converting the hostility into fuel, the whole matchup tilts back toward the bigger cosmic engine.

### 8.3 Turning points
- The first real corruption or possession window that forces Galactus to answer the field, not the body.
- The first successful or failed energy-conversion check on Trigon's hostile output.
- Whether home-realm control actually distorts the fight enough to stop Galactus from normalizing the space.
- The point where Galactus either stabilizes the arena or fails and lets Trigon keep the realm poisonous.

### 8.4 Source verdict distribution
- **explicit**: The long-running ComicVine battle thread `Trigon vs Galactus` (`https://comicvine.gamespot.com/forums/battles-7/trigon-vs-galactus-560406/`) leaned Galactus on neutral assumptions, arguing that his larger scale and feeding logic were the safer default.
- **derived**: Fresh feat synthesis for this paper still lands on Galactus as the baseline favorite, but only so long as satiation and neutral-space stability stay on his side of the exchange.

## 9. Minority perspective
Trigon's best route does not require him to out-stat Galactus. It requires him to make the battlefield itself unreadable and hostile before Galactus can metabolize it. In the right field state, Trigon's hax matters more than the raw averages suggest.

## 10. Base verdict
**Galactus, 7/10.** He owns the broader baseline profile, the more reliable neutral-space control, and the cleaner ability to turn hostile conditions into usable pressure. Trigon is dangerous, but he needs the field to stay demonic longer than Galactus needs it to become solvable.

## 11. Conditional verdict
1. **Well-Fed + Neutral:** `Galactus 8/10`  
   This is the cleanest Galactus branch. He has the best energy economy, the least hostile field distortion, and the safest path to outlasting Trigon's early control windows.

2. **Well-Fed + Trigon Realm:** `Galactus 6.5/10`  
   Trigon's home turf gives him better corruption pressure and anti-symmetry hax, but a well-fed Galactus still has the better chance to stabilize and convert the realm before it fully locks.

3. **Starving + Neutral:** `Trigon 6/10`  
   Hunger removes Galactus's safest version of the matchup even off-home turf. Trigon does not need to be bigger here; he only needs the field to stay hostile long enough for his control to matter.

4. **Starving + Trigon Realm:** `Trigon 7.5/10`  
   This is Trigon's best realistic branch. Lower-fed Galactus loses his cleanest energy-economy answer while the battlefield itself starts favoring demonic control and corruption.

## 12. Why stats and verdict can diverge
Trigon's value is nonlinear. Averages capture that he is dangerous, but they do not fully capture how much hostile-field logic can distort a fight. Galactus's bigger profile also does not erase satiation dependence. This matchup is decided by energy economy and battlefield state, not by raw category totals alone.

## 13. Closing
Galactus is the default answer because he is the larger, steadier cosmic system in neutral conditions. Trigon stays live because he attacks the fight state itself, not just the target standing in front of him. The biggest uncertainty is not raw power. It is how quickly Galactus can turn a demonic realm from poison into fuel.
