Saitama was once an ordinary man like you or me, trying to get by in a 9-to-5 world and make ends meet. One day, after a failed job interview left him depressed and hopeless, he ran into the monster Crablante, and things changed. Having always wanted to be a hero as a kid, Saitama began to train hard, and as a result, two things happened. One, his hair fell out, and two, he became so strong that he could kill any monster with just one punch.

Which, honestly, really sucked.

Since becoming strong, Saitama was able to be a hero as a hobby, but his overwhelming power denied him what he wanted most: a real fight where he could go all-out and push the limits of his strength. What's more, the world at large didn't seem to acknowledge his power, dropping him at the bottom of the hero ranks and attributing most of his efforts to others. In that time, though, he's met and taught the cyborg Genos, encountered all sorts of incredible monsters like Boros, Orochi and Garou, and prevented massive cataclysms more than once. And, though he doesn't make a big deal of it, he's got some pretty good heroic instincts. Maybe this hero thing will work out after all.

Fun Facts About Io and Jupiter

Io is a little bit larger than Earth's moon, which is about a quarter of the size of the Earth and about as wide as the continental United States.

Io's surface temperature is around -202 degrees Fahrenheit or -130 degrees Celsius. The volcanic regions (which Saitama may have come in contact with while bouncing around in its pieces) can reach 3,000 degrees Fahrenheit or 1,649 degrees Celsius.

Io is the fifth moon from Jupiter, orbiting around 262,000 miles or 422,000 kilometers away from the planet.

Io is primarily composed of sulfur, and its atmosphere is both incredibly thin (about one billionth the surface pressure of Earth's atmosphere) and is composed of sulfur dioxide. Sulfur dioxide is most commonly associated with the burning of coal, oil, or diesel fuels and is toxic to humans.

Jupiter is between 365 million and 601 million miles away from Earth, depending on how their orbits line up.

Scientists aren't entirely sure about the exact composition of Jupiter, but it's generally understood that Jupiter's atmosphere is mainly ~90% hydrogen and ~10% helium holding together other elements such as ammonia, sulfur, methane, and water vapor. The core could either be molten liquid or solid rock - information on Jupiter's core is sparse and it hasn't been fully understood yet.

Limitless Growth: Saitama's growth has no cap because of the removed limiter. Though his passive growth is impressive enough for Saitama to easily beat his yesterday's self, when fighting seriously, he is capable of multiplying his existing power by a rapidly expanding factor with every punch even while fighting at MFTL+ speeds.

Potential Toonforce: Though Saitama being a Toonforce character has not been full-fledged or widely accepted, it is still a fact that Saitama possesses a variety of abilities currently impossible to explain in-verse. Whether or not one decides to accept them as actual Toonforce is up to an individual. The fact is, Saitama can freely ignore a selection of laws of physics, for example, sneeze and talk while outside of Earth's atmosphere, fart his way around solar system, move around cracks in spacetime as if they were objects, seemingly appear instantly wherever he chooses to be, bypass invisibility and intangibility, or affect, cross and damage other dimensions of reality, freely perceiving and interacting with spiritual entities if he chooses to, and more.
